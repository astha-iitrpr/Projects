Vegetable Pakora recipe

Here’s a delightful Vegetable Pakora recipe that you can enjoy as a snack or with a cup of chai. These crispy fritters are made with mixed vegetables, spices, herbs, and gram flour.
Let’s get cooking!

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup besan (gram flour, more if needed)
  • ¼ cup rice flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 green chili peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons mint leaves (or coriander leaves)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • ½ teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Oil for deep frying (as needed)
  • 1 medium carrot
  • ¼ cup capsicum
  • 1 cup cabbage
  • 1 medium onion
  • 6 French beans
  • 1 cup spinach (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour, salt, green chili peppers, mint leaves, ginger garlic paste, garam masala powder (if using), and carom seeds.
  2. Gradually add water to form a thick batter. Adjust the consistency as needed.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
  4. Dip the julienned vegetables (carrot, capsicum, cabbage, onion, French beans, and spinach) into the batter, ensuring they are well coated.
  5. Carefully drop the coated vegetables into the hot oil and fry until golden and crisp.
  6. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  7. Serve the vegetable pakoras hot with mint chutney or coriander chutney.
